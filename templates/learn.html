What is Phishing?

Phishing is a cyber attack where attackers try to trick you into giving sensitive information like passwords, credit card numbers, or personal data. They often do this by pretending to be a trustworthy entity in emails, websites, or messages.

Common Signs of Phishing

  • Unsolicited emails asking for personal information
  • URLs that don't match the legitimate website
  • Urgent requests for immediate action
  • Poor grammar or spelling in official-looking communications
  • Requests for sensitive information via email or phone
  • Unexpected attachments or links

How to Protect Yourself

  • Verify the sender's email address
  • Hover over links to see the actual URL
  • Use two-factor authentication
  • Keep your software and antivirus up to date
  • Don't click on suspicious links or download unknown attachments
  • Report suspicious emails to your IT department or email provider

Types of Phishing Attacks

Email Phishing

The most common type, where attackers send fraudulent emails pretending to be from legitimate organizations.

Spear Phishing

Targeted attacks on specific individuals or organizations, using personalized information.

Vishing

Voice phishing over the phone, where attackers call pretending to be from trusted organizations.

Smishing

SMS phishing via text messages, often containing malicious links or urgent requests.
